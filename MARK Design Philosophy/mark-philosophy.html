Fire Gradient — The Field

The orange logo field is not a background — it IS the design. `#ff4500 → #ff2000` as a `linear-gradient(145deg)`. Only used when the entire surface becomes the mark. Never used as a card background or subtle tint — only as a total commitment.

Panther Black — The Absolute

`#0d0d0b` — not pure black, slightly warm. Used for silhouette elements, headlines on white, and the void sections. Never combined with orange on the same surface — they occupy different fields. When black appears, orange disappears.

Terracotta — The Single Accent

The dancer's accent line. `#d4613a` used only on: borders between black/white zones, the active/hover state, and the accent at joints and inflection points. Never used in the fire world or the black world simultaneously. Like the illustration — placed only where the body bends.

The field exists
for the mark alone.

01

Negative space is active, not empty. The white field around the panther is doing as much work as the black form. In MARK, every background is considered from the perspective of the mark it serves — not as a default state, but as a deliberate amplifier.

02

One form. No detail inside it. The panther has no fur, no eye detail, no interior line work. A silhouette is the most confident version of a form — it commits to shape without negotiating texture. In MARK, primary marks are silhouettes: flat, absolute, total.

03

Heraldic implies permanence. The panther rampant posture comes from coat-of-arms tradition — marks meant to last centuries. In MARK, the primary mark always carries that posture: mid-gesture, decisive, facing forward. Never passive. Never symmetric. Always at the moment of action.

04

Scale is the argument. On a large white field, even a small black form carries authority if it is well-placed. The panther needs no caption, no supporting text, no color. The scale relationship between mark and field IS the composition.

04 — motion language

Four ways a mark arrives.

Loop Rotation

The interlocking hex rings rotate at different speeds — outer ring slowest, core fastest — creating a perpetual motion illusion that mirrors the impossible Escher-loop geometry of the mark itself. Three layers, three speeds, one mark. Never stops. Never accelerates. Eternal, not frantic.

outer: 12s linear — inner: 8s reverse — core: 5s — all: infinite · no ease
Heraldic Hover

Silhouette marks on hover lift slightly — a 3–4px vertical translation with the shadow beneath compressing and fading, exactly mimicking the physics of something rising off a surface. Not a bounce, not a spring. A single composed lift, as if the beast is deciding whether to leap. Shadow is mandatory.

translateY: 0 → −4px — 2.5s ease-in-out — shadow: scale .7, opacity .25 sync
Joint Accent Pulse

In illustration-derived components, the terracotta accent at joints — elbows, knees, connection points — pulses gently: opacity .6 → 1 → .6. The rest of the figure remains static. Only the points of articulation are alive. This creates the sense of movement without animation of the full form.

joint dots: opacity .6→1→.6 — 2–3s ease-in-out — stagger 200ms per joint
one mark · one field
Single Mark Placement

On page load, the primary mark doesn't appear — it arrives. Scale from `.85` to `1`, with a single ring of attention radiating outward and fading. No bounce, no overshoot. The mark arrives at its exact position with total composure, and then it is still. The ring confirms arrival, then disappears.

scale: .85→1 — 0.9s cubic-bezier(.16,1,.3,1) — ring: scale 1→1.4, opacity 1→0, 600ms

Black + white +
one line.

01

Black fill, no interior detail. Illustration forms are flat silhouette — no shading, no texture, no hatching. The silhouette defines the character entirely. If the shape isn't legible on its own, redraw the shape.

02

Terracotta only at joints and shadows. The accent color `#d4613a` is used exclusively at points of articulation (elbows, knees, ankles) and at the ground shadow beneath a figure. Never used as fill, never used on garments, never used for highlights.

03

The ground shadow is always terracotta, never black. Where a figure or object meets its surface, the shadow is a soft `rgba(212,97,58,0.20–0.30)` ellipse. This connects illustration language to accent language — the ground is where terracotta lives.

04

White outline lines are used only for separation. When two black forms overlap, a thin white `1px` line separates them — creating the illusion of depth without gradients. The Pulp Fiction illustration uses this at suit edges. In MARK, this is the only permitted form of layering depth.

05

Ground is off-white, never pure white in illustration contexts. `#fafaf8` creates the paper feel that makes the ink marks warmer and more intentional. Pure white reads as digital. Off-white reads as chosen.

05 — design principles

Five laws of the mark.

01
The field has
one purpose.

The orange gradient field in the hex logo exists entirely to amplify the white mark. The white background of the panther image exists entirely to amplify the black silhouette. In MARK, no background is neutral — every field is actively working to make the mark more absolute. If the field has nothing to amplify, it should not exist.

02
Commitment is
the only style.

The orange logo is 100% orange. The panther is 100% black. There is no halfway — no gradient easing into white, no opacity reducing the mark's presence, no supporting illustration softening the impact. In MARK, partial commitment reads as uncertainty. A mark placed at full opacity, full color, and full scale is not aggressive — it is honest.

03
The accent marks
where the body bends.

Terracotta in the Pulp Fiction illustration appears only where a joint flexes — where the body is doing something. In MARK, the terracotta accent is used only at transition points: the moment a section changes, the hover state, the active indicator, the border between two states. It marks inflection, never decoration.

04
Recursion is
the highest form.

The hex logo is an interlocking loop — a mark that contains itself, that passes through itself, that has no clear beginning or end. In MARK, the most sophisticated marks contain their own logic as a visible structure. The loop that generates the form IS the concept being communicated. The form is the argument.

05
Heraldic means
built to last centuries.

The panther rampant is a posture designed for coats of arms meant to identify houses across generations. In MARK, primary marks and icons are designed with that permanence in mind — no current trend, no motion-dependent meaning, no color-dependent legibility. If it doesn't work as a single flat black shape on white, it isn't a mark yet.
